What this is all about...

It's all about

People I see...

For some reason, I gravitate towards street people. When I see them, I wonder about how they ended up existing on the street. I want to hear their stories and get to know them. Seeing street people makes me question our society and why we allow them to live without shelter in such a wealthy country.

It's all about

Places I see...

Old buildings, factories, and houses always seem to catch my eye. I feel some unexplained connection and attraction to them, while modern steel and glass structures somewhat repulse me. Maybe we could use some of these old abandoned places to provide livable shelter for our street people.

It's all about

Things I see...

Another unexplained attraction of mine is random discarded items left on the street or in an alley. These items have ranged from old shoes to a stand up piano laying on its side in Seattle. I can see how most of these random items were simply abandoned, but the sideways piano is still a mystery to me.

How it all began

My Photography Story

The methods behind the madness

I never considered photography something that I would pursue or ever be into. However, I did own a cheap point and shoot camera that I used to document projects, but I had zero clue about how to properly use it. I didn’t know anything about the principals of photography or composition, nor did I know the names or works from well known photographers.

While I was going to school I needed an art credit, so I thought I would use my cheap camera and take a beginning photography class. My classmates all had nice, expensive, DSLR cameras and seemed to be very knowledgeable about all of the things in photography that I wasn’t: proper camera functions, editing software, composition, artsy famous photographers etc.. The class discussions where about camera tech stuff or who was doing a show at some stuffy gallery and it all sounded very foreign to me. Basically I was the dumb kid in the class. Being somewhat spiteful to mask my feelings of photographic inadequacies, I set out to lean as much as possible about the tech side of camera use and the principals of photography. Setting out with determination and a $60.00 point and shoot camera, I started my journey to take an interesting photo.

Since that first class, I’ve purchased a couple of really good cameras, become fairly good with editing software and know the names of a few photographers. Far as taking interesting photos, sometimes I get lucky and other times I don’t. It all depends on how in in tuned I am with my surroundings and what the photo gods provide me with.

Gallery One...

People from the streets of Portland, Oregon

Previous Next

This group of images is comprised of people that I shot (with my camera) on the streets of Portland Oregon from 2012 to 2016. Sadly, over the last decade there has been an increasing population of people living on the streets of Portland, and the city doesn’t seem to be working on a viable solution to the rapidly growing homeless problem.

Gallery Two...

Places I discovered in the Pacific Northwest

Previous Next

Traveling around the Pacific Northwest, I’ve found some very unusual and/or interesting (at least to me they are) places. They could be anything from a crumbling factory to a distinctive store-front. Regardless of what they may be, they have all inspired me to capture them with my camera so I can see them again or share the moment with other people.
