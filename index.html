Street Photography

Assorted Images By: Richard Long

What it's all about...

It's all about

People I see...

A compelling force draws me to those experiencing homelessness, sparking a deep curiosity about the unique paths that brought them to the streets. I find myself eager to engage with these individuals, listening intently to the intricacies of their stories and seeking connections that go beyond casual encounters. In a nation blessed with wealth and resources, the enduring presence of people without permanent shelter raises poignant questions about the societal structures allowing such circumstances. How can a society with ample means not guarantee every individual a place to call home?

It's all about

Places I see...

Ancient structures, factories, and homes captivate my attention, evoking an unexplained connection and attraction within me. The allure of these weathered edifices contrasts sharply with my aversion to souless, modern steel and glass constructions. I find myself envisioning a transformative purpose for these abandoned places, contemplating their potential as livable shelters for those experiencing homelessness. Perhaps repurposing these aged structures could offer a unique solution to provide refuge for our street community.

It's all about

Things I see...

I often find myself drawn to inexplicable attractions, one of which is the allure of random discarded items scattered on the streets or tucked away in alleys. From worn-out shoes to a toppled stand-up piano in the heart of Seattle, these remnants of the past stir a sense of intrigue within me. As I contemplate the stories behind these abandoned belongings, it becomes evident that most items were left behind with a purpose lost to time. However, the enigma surrounding the sideways piano persists, leaving me with a lingering sense of curiosity and wonder.

How it all began

My Photography Story

Located in SE Portland, OR

Photography never interested me as either an art form or a pursuit. Somehow, I stumbled upon a budget-friendly point-and-shoot camera, a technological marvel that, regrettably, I had zero clues on operating. During my scholarly escapades and in desperate need of an art credit, I made the groundbreaking decision to enroll in a photography class to decipher the mysteries of said camera—a choice that, against all odds, stands as one of the most brilliant in my repertoire. Let me emphasize once more: my camera skills were so pitiful that transferring photos from the camera to my laptop resembled a cryptic ritual beyond my intellectual grasp.

The first day of photography class was miserable, surpassed by the subsequent days two, three, and four. My classmates, armed with proper cameras, deemed my attempts at picture-taking a photographic tragedy, turning me into the class pariah. Presenting my abysmal pictures for critique was mortifying, leading me to vow to outshine my seemingly superior peers. Fueled by spite, I acquired a battered camera, challenging myself to unlock its mysteries and improve my ability to visualize photos before capturing them.

Since that spite-infused epiphany, I've undergone substantial improvement. I've compiled a couple of books, graced a few shows with my presence, and even managed to part with some of my artistic creations for a fee. In my unparalleled generosity, I aspire to transform "Art of Spite" into a haven for those enamored with the art of losing oneself in photographic endeavors – because why not revel in the beauty of chaos? Cheers to creating masterpieces out of spiteful endeavors!
